# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

## Overview

OctoAcme's project management processes are structured to ensure rigorous organization, effective collaboration, and continuous improvement across cross-functional teams. At the core, OctoAcme emphasizes an iterative approach, centering on customer value, clear ownership, and data-informed decision-making. Every project starts with a well-defined initiation phase, using templates such as the Project One-pager to clarify problem statements, objectives, success metrics, stakeholder alignment, and resource planning. The planning process translates strategic goals into actionable backlogs, with prioritized and estimated work items, explicit acceptance criteria, and a documented Definition of Done to guide both delivery and quality.

Key roles within OctoAcme are clearly delineated to support efficiency and alignment. Project Managers facilitate delivery roadmaps, risk management, scheduling, and communication, while Product Managers drive product vision, backlog prioritization, and outcome measurement. Developers focus on building, testing, and documenting software, collaborating closely with PMs and Product Managers. QA roles are integrated into the lifecycle, ensuring that features meet acceptance criteria and project documentation—ranging from risk registers to release notes—remains up to date. Communication between these personas is maintained through daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, monthly stakeholder updates, and dedicated channels for ad-hoc escalations.

Execution and tracking depend on structured workflows. Projects are managed via a GitHub Projects board, moving work through standardized columns like Backlog, In Progress, QA, and Done. Pull requests are kept small when possible, requiring automated test and lint checks as well as peer review before merging. Quality assurance is multi-layered, including unit, integration, and end-to-end tests, with manual QA and security scanning supplementing automated checks. Metrics such as velocity, burndown, and system health signals are tracked to guide reporting and target successful delivery.

Risk management and continuous improvement are woven into OctoAcme's culture. Risks and dependencies are systematically tracked and escalated as needed, using a documented risk register and defined escalation paths. Post-release retrospectives and incident reviews are regular practices, aimed at identifying what went well and what can be improved. Action items from retrospectives are added to the backlog for accountability, supporting a cycle of continuous learning and process refinement. This disciplined blend of structured workflow, well-defined roles, and a strong feedback loop underlies OctoAcme's project management excellence.

## Project Management Process Summary

- **Initiation:** Validate needs, align stakeholders, and outline deliverables using clear success metrics and one-pager templates.
- **Planning:** Break down work into actionable increments, identify dependencies, and establish timelines, milestones, and risk registers.
- **Execution & Tracking:** Use project boards, daily standups, and review rituals to drive progress, surface blockers, and ensure quality through testing and metrics.
- **Release & Deployment:** Standardized release process—with checklists for pre-release, deployment, and rollback—to minimize risk and maximize traceability.
- **Risk Management & Communication:** Maintain risk registers, assess and communicate risks, and follow escalation paths.
- **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement:** Capture learnings post-release or incident, driving actionable improvements and celebrating small wins.
- **Roles & Personas:** Clearly defined responsibilities for PMs, PdMs, Developers, QA, and Stakeholders.
